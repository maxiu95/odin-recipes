My Favourite Beans on Toast

Description

Now, online I hear you all dissin' British food. It's bland, boring, where's the seasoning?? lol... But come on man, British food is some of the best. It may not be as exciting as a spicy curry or iconic as the simple tomato pasta, but trust me, it has it's place.

British food is simple. It's for the working man. It goes with a nice mug of strong tea and keeps you going. It's heartwarming and feels like a nice hug on a rainy day. You might all laugh at us, but it's what keeps us going through the day.

The biggest hug on a plate for me has to be beans on toast. It's perfect for any meal, any time of the day. If you can get ahold of a nice can of Heinz (beans means Heinz) or Branston beans definitely give it a try.

This is my favourite arrangement of the Best of British, beans on toast.

Ingredients

  • A can of baked beans
  • Brown bread (the more seeds and that the better)
  • Salted butter
  • Strong mature chedder cheese
  • Marmite
  • Black pepper to season

Steps

  1. Get the tin of beans in a pan and start heating on a low to medium heat.
  2. Bread in the toaster ticking away
  3. Once the toast has popped spread a generous amount of your favourite salted butter. Make sure to reach all the corners and edges of the toast.
  4. Spread a nice layer of marmite over top of the butter. I like a nice layer but put as little or as much as you like.
  5. Layer thin slices of the chedder on both slices of toast.
  6. Pour the beans all over the top, season with some black pepper and enjoy.
